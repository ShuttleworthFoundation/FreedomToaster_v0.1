Getting Started with Linux

Linux is the name of a powerful operating system that you can run on your home or business PC.

It is the system used and developed by scientists, researchers, IT professionals and hobbyists. It is industrial strength computing for the ordinary user.

Linux is the system that someone gave to Mark Shuttleworth, which enabled him to build a multi-million Rand business from scratch. That is why the Shuttleworth Foundation is making Linux accessible to all through this Freedom Toaster.

Linux comes bundled with programs and utilities and themes. These bundles are called distributions (or distros for short). Like when you buy a Windows PC, you still need to buy MS Office and anti-virus utilities and things. Those things all come included with a linux distro.

This kiosk can burn any of a number of linux distros, from those that come on 1 CD to those that come on 13 CDs (that is a lot of software!). The next few screens will help you decide which distro you might like to try.
